# Legal Correspondence Drafter Skill

## Purpose
Draft professional legal correspondence appropriate for Ontario law practice, with proper formatting, tone, and legal references.

## Letter Types

### 1. Demand Letter
```
[FIRM LETTERHEAD]

[Date]

WITHOUT PREJUDICE

SENT VIA [EMAIL / REGISTERED MAIL / COURIER]

[Recipient Name]
[Address]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name]:

Re: [Client Name] v. [Recipient/Company]
    Our File: [File Number]

We are counsel for [Client Name] regarding [brief description of matter].

[FACTS — chronological, objective, 2-4 paragraphs]

[LEGAL BASIS — applicable law, 1-2 paragraphs]
Our client's position is supported by [statute/case law reference].

[DEMAND — specific, measurable, time-limited]
We hereby demand that you [specific action] within [X] days of the
date of this letter, failing which our client will [consequence].

Our client's claim includes:
  (a) [Amount/item 1]
  (b) [Amount/item 2]
  (c) Pre-judgment interest pursuant to the Courts of Justice Act
  (d) Costs of this proceeding

[SETTLEMENT INVITATION]
Our client is prepared to resolve this matter without litigation if
[conditions]. We invite you to respond within [X] days.

Please govern yourself accordingly.

Yours truly,

[Lawyer Name]
[Firm Name]

cc: [Client Name]
Encl: [List of enclosures]
```

### 2. Without Prejudice Settlement Letter
```
[FIRM LETTERHEAD]

[Date]

STRICTLY WITHOUT PREJUDICE
SETTLEMENT COMMUNICATION — NOT ADMISSIBLE

[Opposing Counsel Name]
[Firm Name]
[Address]

Dear Counsel:

Re: [Style of Cause]
    Court File No.: [Number]
    Our File: [Number]
    Your File: [Number]

Further to [recent event/communication], our client instructs us
to make the following offer to settle:

[OFFER TERMS — clear, specific, numbered]
1. Payment of $[amount] inclusive of / plus costs
2. [Other terms]
3. Full and final release

This offer is open for acceptance until [date/time].

[RATIONALE — brief, persuasive]
Our client makes this offer having regard to [strengths of position]
while acknowledging [practical considerations].

[COST CONSEQUENCES — if applicable]
We draw your attention to the cost consequences under
[Rule 49.10 / Small Claims Rule 14.07].

We look forward to your client's response.

Yours truly,

[Lawyer Name]
```

### 3. Reporting Letter to Client
```
[FIRM LETTERHEAD]

[Date]

PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL
SOLICITOR-CLIENT COMMUNICATION

[Client Name]
[Address]

Dear [Client first name]:

Re: [Matter description]
    Our File: [Number]

[PURPOSE — why writing]
I am writing to update you on the status of your matter.

[UPDATE — what has happened since last communication]

[ANALYSIS — our assessment]

[OPTIONS — laid out clearly for client decision]
You have the following options:
  Option 1: [Description, pros, cons, estimated cost]
  Option 2: [Description, pros, cons, estimated cost]
  Option 3: [Description, pros, cons, estimated cost]

[RECOMMENDATION]
Based on our assessment, we recommend [option] because [reasons].

[NEXT STEPS — what happens next, what client needs to do]
Please let us know how you would like to proceed by [date].

[FEES UPDATE — if appropriate]
To date, our fees are approximately $[amount]. We estimate
[remaining work] will cost approximately $[range].

Please do not hesitate to contact our office with any questions.

Yours truly,

[Lawyer Name]
```

## Tone Guidelines

| Correspondence Type | Tone |
|---------------------|------|
| Demand letter | Firm, professional, authoritative |
| Settlement letter | Reasonable, pragmatic, solution-oriented |
| Client reporting | Clear, accessible, empathetic |
| To opposing counsel | Professional, courteous, precise |
| To court/tribunal | Respectful, formal, procedurally correct |

## Key Principles
1. **Never threaten criminal proceedings** to gain civil advantage (professional misconduct)
2. **"Without prejudice"** — only use when genuinely making/discussing settlement
3. **"Please govern yourself accordingly"** — use only in demand letters, sparingly
4. **Always copy client** on correspondence to opposing parties
5. **Use proper style of cause** in re: line once litigation commenced
6. **Check LSO rules** on communicating with represented parties (Rule 7.2-6)
7. **Mark privileged** communications to client clearly
