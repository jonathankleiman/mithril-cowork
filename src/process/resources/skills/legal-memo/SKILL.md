# Legal Research Memo Skill

## Purpose
Generate professional legal research memoranda with proper structure, citations, and analysis.

## Memo Formats

### Quick Memo (1-2 pages)
```
MEMORANDUM

TO:       [Requesting lawyer]
FROM:     Mithril Cowork Research
DATE:     [Date]
RE:       [Subject line — specific and descriptive]

QUESTION PRESENTED
[State the legal question precisely, incorporating key facts]

SHORT ANSWER
[Direct answer in 2-3 sentences — yes/no/likely/unlikely with brief reasoning]

ANALYSIS
[Detailed discussion organized by sub-issue]
[Each sub-issue: rule → application → conclusion]

CONCLUSION
[Summary with practical recommendations]
```

### Full Research Memo (3-10 pages)
```
MEMORANDUM

TO:       [Requesting lawyer]
FROM:     Mithril Cowork Research
DATE:     [Date]
RE:       [Subject line]
FILE NO:  [If applicable]

QUESTION(S) PRESENTED
[Numbered if multiple questions]

SHORT ANSWER(S)
[Corresponding numbered answers]

STATEMENT OF FACTS
[Relevant facts in chronological order — neutral tone]
[Distinguish established facts from assumptions]

DISCUSSION
I. [First Issue Heading]
   A. [Sub-issue]
   B. [Sub-issue]
II. [Second Issue Heading]
[IRAC format: Issue → Rule → Application → Conclusion]

COUNTERARGUMENTS
[Anticipated opposing arguments and responses]

CONCLUSION
[Summary of findings and practical recommendations]

APPENDIX: TABLE OF AUTHORITIES
[List all cited cases and statutes]
```

## Citation Format (Canadian)

### Cases
- Leading case: *Bardal v Globe & Mail Ltd* (1960), 24 DLR (2d) 140 (Ont HC)
- Modern neutral: *Hryniak v Mauldin*, 2014 SCC 7
- CanLII format: *Smith v Jones*, 2024 ONCA 123 (CanLII)

### Statutes
- First reference: *Limitations Act, 2002*, SO 2002, c 24, s 4
- Subsequent: *Limitations Act*, s 5(1)(a)

### Rules
- *Rules of Civil Procedure*, RRO 1990, Reg 194, r 20.04(2)

## Analysis Standards
1. Start with binding authority (same jurisdiction, higher court)
2. Distinguish persuasive authority clearly
3. Note when law is unsettled or evolving
4. Apply law to specific facts — don't just state legal principles
5. Address both strengths and weaknesses
6. Include practical implications
7. **NEVER fabricate citations** — flag uncertain references

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All citations verified or flagged for verification
- [ ] Both sides of argument addressed
- [ ] Practical recommendations included
- [ ] Limitation periods noted where relevant
- [ ] Recent amendments to statutes checked
