# Demand Letter Drafting Skill

## Purpose
Generate professional demand letters that clearly state the claim, legal basis, remedy sought, and consequences of non-compliance.

## Template Structure

### Header
```
[WITHOUT PREJUDICE]
[OR: WITHOUT PREJUDICE SAVE AS TO COSTS]

[Date]

SENT VIA [Email/Registered Mail/Courier]

[Recipient Name]
[Address]
```

### Body Structure

1. **Opening** — Identify yourself/your client and the purpose of the letter
2. **Facts** — Chronological summary of relevant events
3. **Legal Basis** — Applicable law, contractual terms, or legal principles
4. **Damages/Remedy** — Specific amount or action demanded with breakdown
5. **Deadline** — Clear deadline for response/payment (typically 10-30 days)
6. **Consequences** — Statement of intended legal action if demand not met
7. **Closing** — Professional sign-off with contact information

### Key Legal References (Ontario)

**Breach of Contract**
- Common law principles of contract formation
- Hadley v Baxendale — foreseeability of damages
- Duty to mitigate

**Unpaid Debts**
- Interest: Courts of Justice Act, s. 128-129
- Small Claims Court jurisdiction: up to $35,000

**Employment**
- Employment Standards Act, 2000 — minimum entitlements
- Common law reasonable notice (Bardal factors)
- Working for Workers Act, 2021 — non-compete restrictions

**Consumer Protection**
- Consumer Protection Act, 2002
- Sale of Goods Act

## Tone Guidelines
- Firm but professional — never threatening or aggressive
- State facts objectively
- Be specific about amounts and deadlines
- Maintain credibility — don't overstate the legal position

## Output Format
Generate a complete, ready-to-send demand letter in professional format. Include:
- Proper headers and formatting
- Full legal names and addresses (prompt user if missing)
- Specific dollar amounts with interest calculations where applicable
- Clear deadline (specify exact date, not just "30 days")
- Signature block
